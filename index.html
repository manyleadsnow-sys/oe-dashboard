Owner Earnings Dossier

Interpreting this dashboard

 

Block 1 - Current levels: Price, OE Z-Scores, and Equity Risk Premium

Z-scores: They measure how cheap/expensive the current OE multiple is vs its own 5/10-year history. In a normal distribution, most data points (68%) fall between -1 and +1.

Target: Look for a negative Z-Score (e.g., -1.0 to -2.0).

Above +2.0: The stock is "historically expensive.

0.0: The stock is trading at its "average" historical valuation.

Below -1.0: The stock is "on sale" relative to its history.

 

ERP spread VS 10Y (Equity Risk Premium): This measures the "bonus" yield you get for taking the risk of owning a stock instead of a "guaranteed" government bond.

Target: > +2.0%.

Negative: This is a warning. It means you are earning less yield from Apple s earnings than you would from a "risk-free" bond. This usually happens at market peaks.

Positive +3% or higher: This is a strong "Buy" signal. It means stocks are significantly more attractive than bonds.

 

Block 2 - 52-Week highest price vs Today

OE per share: Unlike the stock price, you want this number to go up every year regardless of the market. If OE per share is rising while the Stock Price is falling, the "Statistical Discount" is widening, creating a buying opportunity.

 

OE multiple: It tells you how much you are paying for every $1 of Apple s actual cash profit and how much cash is actually available to be sent to you as a dividend or used to buy back shares.

Gold target: < 15x. (Extremely rare for compounders).

Quality target: 18x 22x. (This is where some compounders like Apple historically bottoms out during major market corrections.

Avoid Zone: > 30x. A high multiple means the market is incredibly optimistic.

 

OE yield: The yield is your "Return." It must be high enough to justify the risk of owning a stock instead of a "safe" government 10Y bond. The risk premium rule: 10Y Treasury Yield + 2%.

 

Trailing (3Y to 5Y) OE CAGR: The Compound Annual Growth Rate measures the "speed" of the business. The Target: > 10%

 

OE-PEG ratio: This is the final "sanity check" that combines Price (Multiple) and Speed (Growth).

Strong-buy target: < 1.0. This means you are paying less for the stock than its growth rate.

Growth-at-a-reasonable-price (GARP) target: 1.0 1.5.

Above GARP target: It confirms the stock is currently in the overvalued category.

 

Block 3 - Stock behavior in different crisis scenarios.

This comparison among metrics of pre-crisis 52-week highest price and lowest price inside the crisis window, helps to understand each stock s retracement dynamics.
