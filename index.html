How to Use This for DCA

  • Z-Score (5Y & 10Y): If this is negative (e.g., -1.8), you are buying the compounder at a valuation significantly below its own average. This is the "Statistical Discount."
  • ERP Spread: If this is high (e.g., +2.5%), the stock’s cash yield is significantly more attractive than the "Risk-Free" rate of the 10-Year Treasury.
  • Premium to Floor: If this is low (e.g., 8.0%), you are buying within 8% of the lowest valuation the company hit in the last decade of panics. This provides a "Margin of Safety."
