How to Use This for DCA

  • Z-Score (5Y & 10Y): If negative (e.g., -1.8), you are buying the compounder at a valuation significantly below its own historical average. This is the "Statistical Discount."
  • ERP Spread: If high (e.g., +2.5%), the stock's OE yield is significantly more attractive than the 10-Year Treasury "Risk-Free" rate.
  • Premium to Floor: If low (e.g., 8.0%), you are buying within 8% of the lowest valuation the company reached during past crises — a margin-of-safety anchor.
  • Trailing OE CAGR: In each crisis block, the left column shows the trailing growth rate as of the peak date and the right column shows it as of the trough date. Different values confirm OE data is anchored correctly to each moment in time.
