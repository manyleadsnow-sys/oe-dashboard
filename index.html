How to Read This Dashboard

  • Block 1 — Statistical Discount: Z-scores measure how cheap/expensive the current OE multiple is vs its own 5/10-year history. ERP Spread = OE yield minus 10Y Treasury yield.
  • Block 2 — 52-Week Peak vs Today: Left column = metrics at the highest price in the past 52 weeks (OE anchored to the most recent fiscal year-end before that peak date). Right = today.
  • Block 3 — Crisis Blocks: Left column = metrics at the pre-crisis 52-week peak (the peak in the year before the crisis began). Right column = metrics at the trough (lowest price inside the crisis window). OE and CAGR are each independently anchored to the latest available fiscal year-end before each date — so the two columns will show different OE when a new annual filing landed between the peak and trough.
  • Trailing OE CAGR: the compound annual growth rate of Owner Earnings anchored to the most recent fiscal year-end available at each respective date. Not a delta between the two columns.
